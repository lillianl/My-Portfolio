What's in Your Bag?


'What's in Your Bag' is an ongoing series that showcases the personal items of people who I meet while I travel and work. The contents of someone's purse or backpack are highly personal and can tell a lot about not only a someone's tastes or preferences, but also about habits and behavior. The next issue will focus on travels in Denmark and Sweden.


Exchanges


Exchanges was created as a mini-zine for the San Jose Zine Con. Inside lies a collection of poetry and illustration aimed towards addressing empathy, especially after the 2016 elections. Here, we tried to communicate the experiences in our lives that have promopted us to question and modify ideas/ingrained beliefs that we've had since we were young and how these exchanges between our past and present selves have only helped us become more empathetic people.

I.
I get caught in the hills these days, among the trees and mist. The mist blurs things exterior to the space.

II.
Sometimes I can’t even keep my own soul in order; how do I then order the world? Maybe in caring I can heal myself, or in healing myself I can care.

III.
You request that I comply. It seems, you think, the best way to keep everything together, to step along, so that, even if things become worse in part, they get better on the whole. I am here to tell you that I understand. I’ve been in the vicinity of your fear and your frustration. But I no longer dwell in that place. I don’t make my home in those glances over the shoulder to a long-gone past softened as the tears dry, as the edge wears down. I am here. I see this place around me, still blazing in my eyes because for so long I’ve thrown my arms across my face in fear.

IV.
To remain silent is to become a monster sometimes, isn’t it? I didn’t know that routine could make me a monster, that habit could make me a monster, that prudence could make me a monster.

V.
I won’t be strong for you; what I can muster can’t really be called strength, if I’m honest. But I will stand with you in all my weakness. I will learn again how to speak and work and burrow into the malaise and fear: past those and to the heart of things.

-Austin Lim
